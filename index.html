CardPilot Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-02-16

App: CardPilot (Business Card Scanner & Contact Manager)

Plain-language summary

  • CardPilot is designed to keep your scanned business cards primarily on your device.
  • When you choose, you can export a card to your device’s Contacts app (“Create Contact”).
  • If you enable ads, CardPilot uses Google AdMob, which may collect device identifiers and usage data to serve ads.
  • If you enable cloud sync in the future (e.g., iCloud/CloudKit), we’ll update this policy and clearly explain what changes.

1) Who we are

This Privacy Policy explains how CardPilot (“CardPilot”, “we”, “us”) handles information when you use the CardPilot mobile application (the “App”).

2) Information CardPilot processes

A. Information you provide (business card content)

CardPilot can capture and store information from business cards you scan, such as names, phone numbers, email addresses, company names, job titles, websites, and addresses (“Card Data”).

B. Images and OCR text

When you scan a card, the App may store:

  • The card image (photo/scan)
  • Text recognized from the image using on-device OCR (“OCR Text”)
  • Structured fields parsed from OCR Text (e.g., email, phone)

C. Device permissions

CardPilot may request the following permissions:

  • Camera – to scan business cards.
  • Contacts – only if you use “Create Contact” to save a card to your device Contacts.
  • Tracking (optional) – if you allow it, AdMob may use the AppTrackingTransparency permission for personalized advertising.

3) How we use information

We use the information described above to:

  • Scan and recognize text from business cards
  • Organize and display your saved cards inside the App
  • Allow you to edit card details
  • Allow you to export to your device Contacts (when you choose)
  • Operate the App, troubleshoot issues, and improve reliability
  • Show ads (if the ad-supported version is enabled and you have not purchased “Remove Ads”)

4) Where your data is stored

On-device storage: By default, CardPilot stores your Card Data on your device (for example, in an internal database on your phone).

Contacts export: If you choose “Create Contact,” the selected card details are saved to your device’s Contacts database. That data is then governed by your device and Apple account settings (including any iCloud Contacts sync you may have enabled).

Cloud sync (future feature): If CardPilot adds optional cloud sync (e.g., iCloud/CloudKit), we will update this policy before enabling the feature and explain what is uploaded, how it’s protected, and how to disable it.

5) Advertising (Google AdMob)

CardPilot may use Google AdMob to display banner and/or interstitial ads. AdMob may automatically collect information such as:

  • Device identifiers (e.g., Advertising Identifier, if available)
  • IP address (which can indicate general location)
  • App interactions and ad performance data
  • Diagnostics data

AdMob’s data practices are governed by Google’s policies. You can learn more in Google’s privacy documentation.

Personalized vs. non-personalized ads: Where required, you may be asked for consent (for example, via Apple’s AppTrackingTransparency prompt). If you decline tracking permission, ads may be less personalized.

Remove Ads: If you purchase “Remove Ads,” CardPilot will stop showing AdMob ads in the App.

6) Analytics

CardPilot does not require you to create an account and is designed to function without collecting personal analytics about your Card Data. If we add optional analytics in the future (for example, to understand feature usage), we will update this policy and provide controls where appropriate.

7) Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell your Card Data.

We may share information in the following limited cases:

  • With service providers: If ads are enabled, AdMob may receive device/usage information as described above.
  • Legal reasons: If required to comply with law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable government request.
  • Safety and protection: To protect rights, safety, and security of users, the public, or our services.

8) Data retention

CardPilot retains your Card Data on your device until you delete it from the App (or delete the App). If you export data to Contacts, you can remove it through your device’s Contacts app.

9) Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information processed by the App. However, no method of storage or transmission is 100% secure. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your device (e.g., device passcode, biometric lock).

10) Children’s privacy

CardPilot is not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

11) Your choices

  • Delete data: You can delete cards within the App. You can also remove the App to delete locally stored App data.
  • Contacts access: You can enable/disable Contacts permission in iOS Settings.
  • Tracking permission: You can allow or deny tracking in iOS Settings (if shown) and still use the App.
  • Remove Ads: You can purchase “Remove Ads” to stop ads from showing.

12) International users

CardPilot is intended to work globally. If third-party services (such as AdMob) are enabled, data may be processed on servers outside your country. Those services may transfer and process data according to their own policies and applicable law.

13) Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date and, where appropriate, provide notice within the App or via an updated policy link.
